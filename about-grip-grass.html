Why Grip Grass?

About

Sandburs

Also known as grip grass, Sandburs are weedy annuals or perennials, typically less than 100 cm (40 inches) tall. The plants are usually shallow-rooted and spread readily. The leaf blades are generally somewhat twisted and feature a fringed ligule (small appendage) at the leaf base. Sandbur flower spikelets are enclosed in sharp burs that readily detach from the plant when ripe.

Choosing a Path

We often come to forks in our lives, where we ust chose one path or another. In a perfect world, every decision would be between two opposite paths (a good one, and a bad one). But in the real world there are an unending number of nuances. So, we pick the "best". The one whee the pros outweigh the cons. But what happens when the options are limiting? Do we resign to what's laid before us? No. It's time to forge a new path, one that's not immediately apparent. We do it all the time, but we sometimes forget that when you forge a new path there's always the unexpected consequence. The grip grass. If you start that path and the grip grass takes you by suprise, it's only natural to want to get back onto the traveled one, but those sticky seeds in your foot are proof of the progress you've made. They're likely one of the smallest obstacles you will encounter on your journey, but they're the most important step. Get a little uncomfortable, get a little lost, and find your own path. If we all create new paths when the old ones aren't suited, our grandchildren will have countless paths to chose from. They might still have some grip grass too, but they can see the footprints. This is how we improve opportunity for all. Don't say "no" to yourself. Run through the grip grass.
